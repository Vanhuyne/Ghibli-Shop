About Studio Ghibli

Studio Ghibli is a renowned Japanese animation film studio headquartered in Tokyo, Japan. It was founded in 1985 by directors Hayao Miyazaki and Isao Takahata, along with producer Toshio Suzuki.

Studio Ghibli is known for its captivating storytelling, breathtaking animation, and imaginative worlds. The studio has produced a wide range of critically acclaimed and beloved animated films that have garnered international recognition and awards.

The studio's name "Ghibli" is derived from the Arabic word for "hot desert wind," chosen by Hayao Miyazaki to represent the studio's desire to blow a new wind through the anime industry.
